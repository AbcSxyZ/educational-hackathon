# Bibliography on Educational Hackathon

## Articles

Gama, K., Alencar, B., Calegario, F., Neves, A., \& Alessio, P. (2018). A Hackathon Methodology for Undergraduate Course Projects. *Federal University of Pernambuco*. [https://doi.org/10.1109/fie.2018.8659264](https://doi.org/10.1109/fie.2018.8659264)
   * Summary: Propose a hackathon methodology for undergraduate course projects, enhancing student engagement and promoting collaborative problem-solving in educational settings.

Warner, J., \& Guo, P. J. (2017). Examining How College Hackathons Are Perceived By Student Attendees and Non-Attendees. *Proceedings of the 2017 ACM Conference on International Computing Education Research*. [https://doi.org/10.1145/3105726.3106174](https://doi.org/10.1145/3105726.3106174)
   * Summary: Investigate the perceptions of college hackathons among student attendees and non-attendees, shedding light on the impact and varied perspectives on these events.

Duhring, J.L. (2014). PROJECT-BASED LEARNING KICKSTART TIPS: Hackathon Pedagogies as Educational Technology. Récupéré sur [https://venturewell.org/open2014/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/DUHRING.pdf](https://venturewell.org/open2014/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/DUHRING.pdf)
   * Summary: Explores hackathon pedagogies as educational technology, offering kickstart tips for project-based learning and incorporating hackathon approaches into educational practices.

Nandi, A., \& Mandernach, M. (2016). Hackathons as an Informal Learning Platform. *Proceedings of the 47th ACM Technical Symposium on Computing Science Education*. [https://doi.org/10.1145/2839509.2844590](https://doi.org/10.1145/2839509.2844590)
   * Summary: Discuss how hackathons serve as informal learning platforms, providing insights into their educational value and impact on participants' skills development.

Kinder, C. J., Gaudreault, K. L., \& Simonton, K. (2020). Structured and unstructured contexts in physical education: Promoting activity, learning and motivation. *Journal of Physical Education, Recreation \& Dance*, *91*(6), 30-35. [https://doi.org/10.1080/07303084.2020.1768181](https://doi.org/10.1080/07303084.2020.1768181)
   * Summary: Examines structured and unstructured contexts in physical education, focusing on promoting activity, learning, and motivation in diverse educational settings.

Persaud, C. (2023, août 17). *Bloom\& # 8217 ; s Taxonomy : The Ultimate Guide [Free Download]*. Top Hat. [https://tophat.com/blog/blooms-taxonomy/](https://tophat.com/blog/blooms-taxonomy/)
   * Summary: Presents an ultimate guide to Bloom's Taxonomy, offering a free download resource for educators seeking a comprehensive understanding of this pedagogical framework.

Government Accountability Office (GAO) (2012). Designing evaluations. (GAO-12-208G). Washington, DC: U.S. Government Printing Office. [http://www.gao.gov/assets/590/588146.pdf](http://www.gao.gov/assets/590/588146.pdf)
   * Summary: Introduces guidelines on designing evaluations, providing a resource for planning and conducting evaluations in various organizational contexts.

W.K. Kellogg Foundation (2004) Introduction to logic models, Creating a basic logic model for your program, Logic model development guide: Using logic models to bring together planning, evaluation, and action., pp. 1-25. Récupéré sur [https://wkkf.issuelab.org/resource/logic-model-development-guide.html](https://wkkf.issuelab.org/resource/logic-model-development-guide.html)
   * Summary: Offers an introduction to logic models and a guide for developing logic models to enhance planning, evaluation, and action in program development.

Porras, J., Knutas, A., Ikonen, J., Happonen, A., Khakurel, J., \& Herala, A. (2019). Code Camps and Hackathons in Education - literature review and lessons learned. *Proceedings of the . . . Annual Hawaii International Conference on System Sciences*. [https://doi.org/10.24251/hicss.2019.933](https://doi.org/10.24251/hicss.2019.933)
   * Summary: Conducts a literature review on code camps and hackathons in education, extracting lessons learned and insights into their impact on learning and skills development.

Yarmohammadian, M. H., Monsef, S., Javanmard, S. H., Yazdi, Y., \& Rarani, M. A. (2021). The role of Hackathon in education : Can hackathon improve health and medical education ? *Journal of education and health promotion*, *10*(1), 334. [https://doi.org/10.4103/jehp.jehp\_1183\_20](https://doi.org/10.4103/jehp.jehp\_1183\_20)
   * Summary: Explores the role of hackathons in education, specifically focusing on their potential to improve health and medical education.

Hackathons for experiential learning in IS higher education. (2022). *Issues in information systems*. [https://doi.org/10.48009/1\_iis\_2022\_123](https://doi.org/10.48009/1\_iis\_2022\_123)
   * Summary: Discusses the use of hackathons for experiential learning in Information Systems higher education, emphasizing their value in enhancing practical skills and problem-solving abilities
